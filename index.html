Biography


Taylor Swift is a globally celebrated singer and songwriter, known for her deeply personal lyrics, genre crossing sound, and powerful storytelling. Born on December 13, 1989, in Pennsylvania, USA, she began her career in country music before evolving into one of the biggest pop icons of her generation.


With hits like Love Story, Shake It Off, and Anti-Hero, she has won over 500 awards, including 12 Grammys. Her eras from Fearless to 1989 to Midnights showcase her growth not just as an artist, but as a voice for empowerment, reinvention, and resilience.
